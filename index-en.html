0104

Human-controlled automation

Human expertise stays in control. AI extends repeatable business workflows.

Each system is defined, configured and supervised by a human. Local models, custom AI tools and automated workflows then help execute repetitive operational tasks faster:without replacing strategic judgment, creative expertise or final approval.

  • Human objectives and strategy
  • Manual workflow configuration
  • Approved data and clear limits
  • Local or custom AI tools
  • Automated operational tasks
  • Human review and correction
  • Final human approval
  • Traceable business actions
01 · Human

Set the objective

Define the campaign goal, audience and expected result.

→
02 · Human

Configure rules

Set budgets, channels, limits, approved data and decision rules.

→
03 · AI tools

Analyze and organize

Local and custom tools structure data, compare signals and prepare recommendations.

→
04 · Automation

Run the workflow

The system repeats approved tasks such as monitoring, classification and scheduling.

→
05 · Human

Review and approve

A human checks the recommendation, corrects it when needed and authorizes the next step.

→
06 · Result

Execute the action

Approved campaign updates are applied and recorded for measurement.

Example: marketing data is monitored and organized automatically, but campaign objectives, limits, recommendations and final actions remain under human control.

General

11
Can I choose only one service?

Yes. You can choose a single service or combine several services depending on your project. I only recommend the services that are genuinely useful for achieving your objectives.

Do you work alone?

I manage projects directly, but I also work as a duo with my husband, who is a professional photographer and technician specialising in 3D modelling. Together, we provide a broader and more complete range of creative and technical services.

How can we start a project together?

Send me a message explaining what you need, what you want to achieve and any ideas you already have. I will review your request and recommend a practical, cost-effective solution.

Do I need to know exactly which services I need?

No. You can simply describe your project, objectives and current challenges. I will identify which services are relevant and create a clear proposal around them.

Are you based in Switzerland?

Yes. I am based near Yverdon-les-Bains, in the canton of Vaud.

Do you work with clients outside Switzerland?

Yes. Most services can be delivered remotely, allowing me to work with clients in Switzerland and internationally.

Who do you work with?

I work with small businesses, independent professionals, artists, brands, manufacturers and established companies that need creative, digital or marketing support.

Can you work within a limited budget?

Yes. Tell me your available budget and priorities, and I will propose the most effective solution within those limits. The objective is not to add unnecessary services, but to focus on what will produce the best result.

Do you provide a quotation before starting?

Yes. Once the scope of the project is clear, you will receive a detailed quotation explaining the services, deliverables, costs and estimated schedule.

How are revisions handled?

The number of revision rounds is defined in the quotation. You will be able to review the project at key stages and provide feedback before final delivery.

Which languages can we use to communicate?

Projects can be discussed and managed in English or French.

Websites

17
How much does a website cost?

Website prices start at CHF 600, including taxes, for a simple website based on an existing template. More advanced or customised websites require a detailed quotation based on the number of pages, features, content and level of custom development required.

What is included in a CHF 600 website?

This starting price is intended for small businesses or independent professionals who need a simple and effective online presence. It generally covers a limited number of pages, a pre-existing design structure, responsive adaptation and basic content integration. The exact scope is confirmed before the project begins.

How long does it take to create a website?

A very small website can sometimes be completed within two or three days when all content is ready. More complex websites generally require between one and four weeks, depending on the number of pages, features, revisions and content preparation.

Do you manage hosting and domain connection?

Yes. I can help you choose a suitable hosting solution, configure the website, connect your domain name and manage the technical launch.

Can you create a professional email address with my domain?

Yes. I can help you configure professional email addresses connected to your domain, such as [contact@yourcompany.com](mailto:contact@yourcompany.com).

Can you create an e-commerce website?

Yes. I can create online shops with products, collections, payment systems, shipping options, customer accounts, contact forms and other necessary e-commerce functions.

Which platforms do you work with?

The platform is selected according to the project. I can work with solutions such as Shopify, WordPress or a custom-developed website when more flexibility is required.

Can you redesign an existing website?

Yes. I can improve the structure, visual identity, content presentation, responsiveness, performance and user experience of an existing website.

Can you develop custom website modules?

Yes. Custom functions can include interactive 3D objects, configurators, planners, calculators, advanced forms, galleries, product viewers and other interactive modules. The feasibility and cost must be assessed before development.

Will my website work on mobile devices?

Yes. Every website is designed or adapted to work properly on desktop computers, tablets and mobile devices.

Can you improve the loading speed of my website?

Yes. I can optimise images, videos, code, page structure and external resources to improve loading performance. The final result also depends on the platform, hosting provider and installed applications.

Can you help me prepare the website content?

Yes. I can help organise the pages, rewrite existing text, create new copy, select images and define the overall content structure.

How does the website creation process work?

We begin with a discussion about your business, audience, objectives and required features. I then recommend the appropriate type of website and send you a quotation. Once approved, I prepare two or three initial design directions. After one direction is selected, I develop the website, share the progress with you and make the agreed revisions before launch.

Will I be able to review the website during development?

Yes. You will receive access to a preview or development version so that you can review the progress and provide feedback at the appropriate stages.

Can I update the website myself after delivery?

Yes. Whenever possible, the website will be structured so that you can update common elements such as text, images, products or articles. Instructions or training can also be provided.

Do you provide website maintenance?

Yes. Monthly or yearly maintenance packages are available. The cost depends on the platform, the expected frequency of updates and the amount of ongoing assistance required.

Do you provide search engine optimisation?

Yes. Basic SEO can include page titles, descriptions, headings, image optimisation, structured content and technical checks. A more advanced SEO strategy can also be developed separately.

Branding

13
What does a branding service include?

Branding can include strategy, positioning, visual direction, logo design, typography, colour palette, graphic elements, brand guidelines and templates for digital or printed communication.

Can you create only a logo?

Yes. A logo can be created as a standalone service. However, for a more coherent result, I may recommend defining at least the main colours, typography and visual direction at the same time.

Can you redesign an existing logo?

Yes. I can modernise, simplify or refine an existing logo while preserving the elements that are already recognisable and important to your brand.

What is the difference between a logo and a complete brand identity?

A logo is one visual symbol representing a business. A complete brand identity defines how the entire brand should look and communicate across websites, social media, documents, packaging, photography and advertising.

Can you create branding for a new business?

Yes. I can help define the visual and strategic foundations of a new brand, even when the business is still at an early stage.

Do you provide several design proposals?

Yes. Depending on the project, I usually present several initial directions before developing the selected concept.

Will I receive the editable logo files?

Yes. Final logo delivery can include vector and image formats suitable for websites, social media, printing and future editing.

Can you create a brand guidelines document?

Yes. The guidelines can explain logo usage, colours, typography, spacing, image direction, graphic elements and examples of correct and incorrect applications.

Can you create social media templates?

Yes. I can create reusable templates for posts, stories, advertisements, announcements, product presentations and other recurring content.

Can you design business cards, brochures or catalogues?

Yes. Printed and digital materials can be developed as part of the identity or as separate design projects.

Can you help define the tone of voice of my brand?

Yes. I can help define how your brand should communicate, including vocabulary, level of formality, key messages, taglines and general writing direction.

Can you create packaging designs?

Yes. I can create the visual design for boxes, labels, sleeves, cards and other packaging elements. Technical production files depend on the specifications supplied by the manufacturer or printer.

How long does a branding project take?

A simple logo project can take several days, while a complete brand identity can require several weeks. The schedule depends on the scope, number of applications and revision process.

3D Rendering

15
What types of 3D rendering do you create?

I create product renders, technical visualisations, advertising images, materials, environments, animations and presentation visuals for sectors such as watches, jewellery, cosmetics, furniture and industrial products.

Do I need to provide a 3D model?

Ideally, you should provide a usable 3D model. I can also offer product modelling, but this work will be quoted separately from the rendering service.

Can you create a 3D model from photographs?

In some cases, yes. Clear photographs, measurements and several viewing angles are required. Complex or highly technical objects may also require plans or CAD files to ensure accuracy.

Which 3D file formats can you use?

Common formats such as STEP, IGES, STL, OBJ, FBX and other standard 3D formats can usually be used. The file should be reviewed before the project begins to confirm compatibility and quality.

Can you create realistic materials and textures?

Yes. I can reproduce materials such as polished metal, brushed metal, leather, glass, gemstones, ceramics, wood, fabric, plastic and painted surfaces.

Can you match an existing product exactly?

I work from the references and technical information provided to reproduce the design as accurately as possible. The quality of the result depends on the precision of the source model, measurements, materials and visual references.

Can you create several colours or material variations?

Yes. Once the main scene is prepared, different product finishes, colours, materials or configurations can often be created efficiently.

Can you place my product in a custom environment?

Yes. The product can be presented in a studio setting, an abstract environment, an architectural scene or a custom advertising composition.

Can you create transparent-background renders?

Yes. Products can be delivered on transparent backgrounds for use in catalogues, websites, advertisements and graphic compositions.

Can you create 360-degree product views?

Yes. I can create turntable animations, interactive image sequences or assets intended for a 360-degree product viewer on a website.

Can you create technical exploded views?

Yes. Exploded views, assembly visualisations, section views and component presentations can be created when the 3D data is sufficiently detailed.

Can you animate a 3D product?

Yes. Animations can include camera movements, product rotations, assembly sequences, material transformations, mechanical movements and cinematic product reveals.

How much does a 3D rendering cost?

Pricing depends on the type of object and its complexity. Once the 3D model has been assembled, textured and approved, each additional image becomes significantly faster and less expensive to produce. For watchmaking projects, the starter package begins at CHF 800 after a project review, as the final price depends on product complexity and the required deliverables.

How long does a 3D project take?

A straightforward product image may take a few days. More complex modelling, environments or animations can require several weeks.

Can the 3D visuals be used for advertising?

Yes. Final renders can be prepared for websites, social media, catalogues, presentations, print campaigns, online advertising and video production.

Photography

12
What types of photography do you offer?

We provide product photography, commercial photography, jewellery and watch photography, corporate portraits, lifestyle images and visual content for websites, catalogues and social media.

Do you specialise in product photography?

Yes. We have experience photographing detailed products that require precise lighting, controlled reflections and careful retouching, particularly watches, jewellery and premium objects.

Can I send my products to you?

Yes. Products can be sent for photography or combined photography and video production. Shipping, insurance, handling and return arrangements must be agreed before sending valuable objects.

Can you photograph reflective objects such as watches or jewellery?

Yes. Reflective products require specialised lighting, positioning and retouching. We use controlled techniques to manage metal, glass, gemstones and polished surfaces.

Do you retouch the photographs?

Yes. Standard retouching can include exposure correction, colour correction, dust removal, background cleaning and general image refinement. Advanced retouching can be included when required.

Can you remove or replace the background?

Yes. Products can be photographed on a clean background, isolated for transparency or integrated into another visual environment.

Can you create focus-stacked macro photographs?

Yes. Focus stacking can be used for small and detailed objects when greater depth of field is needed.

Can you produce both horizontal and vertical images?

Yes. The shooting plan can include formats adapted to websites, catalogues, Instagram posts, stories, advertisements and other platforms.

How many photographs will I receive?

The number of final edited images is defined before the session and included in the quotation.

Do you provide the original RAW files?

RAW files are not normally included in the standard delivery. Final selected and professionally edited images are delivered in the agreed formats. RAW delivery can be discussed separately when necessary.

Can photography and 3D rendering be combined?

Yes. Photography can be combined with 3D elements, digital environments, visual effects or rendered products to create more advanced advertising images.

How long does photo delivery take?

Delivery time depends on the number of images and the required retouching. A clear schedule is provided in the quotation.

Marketing and Sales

12
What marketing services do you provide?

Services can include marketing strategy, campaign planning, social media content, advertising visuals, newsletters, landing pages, sales materials and performance analysis.

Can you create a content strategy?

Yes. I can define content themes, formats, publication frequency, campaign ideas and the role of each platform in your wider marketing strategy.

Can you create the content as well as the strategy?

Yes. Strategy can be combined with photography, video, 3D rendering, graphic design, copywriting and website content.

Can you help me attract more customers?

Yes. I can improve your presentation, communication, website, campaigns and sales journey to strengthen visibility and make it easier to convert prospects into customers.

Can you help improve my sales process?

Yes. I can review how visitors discover your business, understand your offer, contact you, request a quotation or complete a purchase, then recommend improvements.

Can you create advertising campaigns?

Yes. I can help define the campaign concept, audience, messaging, visual assets, landing pages and content variations. Advertising budgets are paid separately by the client.

Can you create newsletters and email campaigns?

Yes. I can design email templates, structure campaigns, write content and help configure automated or scheduled email sequences.

Can you create a landing page for a campaign?

Yes. Landing pages can be designed around a specific product, service, event, advertisement or lead-generation objective.

Can you help launch a new product or brand?

Yes. A launch can combine branding, product photography, 3D rendering, video, website creation, social content, newsletters and advertising materials.

Do you provide marketing reports?

Yes. Reports can include selected indicators such as website traffic, engagement, audience growth, campaign results, enquiries and conversions, depending on the available tracking data.

Can you work with a small marketing budget?

Yes. I can prioritise the most useful channels and content based on your resources instead of spreading the budget across too many activities.

Do you guarantee sales results?

No. Marketing performance depends on the offer, pricing, market, competition, audience, budget and sales process. I provide strategy, content and optimisation designed to improve your chances of success, but specific results cannot be guaranteed.

AI Automation

14
What is AI automation?

AI automation uses artificial intelligence and connected digital tools to reduce repetitive manual work, organise information and accelerate recurring business processes.

What types of tasks can be automated?

Possible examples include email classification, lead collection, customer follow-ups, document generation, content preparation, reporting, data entry, appointment workflows and internal notifications.

How do you identify what should be automated?

We review your current workflow, identify repeated tasks, measure where time is being lost and select the processes where automation would provide a practical benefit.

Do I need technical knowledge to use the automation?

No. The system can be designed with a simple interface and clear instructions. Training or documentation can be included when necessary.

Can AI automation connect with my existing tools?

In many cases, yes. Automations can connect websites, forms, spreadsheets, email platforms, customer relationship management systems, e-commerce platforms and other compatible services.

Can you create an automated customer enquiry system?

Yes. Enquiries can be collected, organised, categorised and transferred to the appropriate person or system. Automated responses can also be created for clearly defined situations.

Can you automate quotation requests?

Yes. A system can collect project information, organise the request and prepare an initial summary or quotation draft. Final pricing and approval should normally remain under human control.

Can you create an AI chatbot for my website?

Yes. A chatbot can answer common questions, guide visitors, collect information and redirect complex requests to a human contact.

Can AI automate social media publishing?

Yes. Content can be prepared, organised and scheduled automatically. However, the strategy and final content should still be reviewed to ensure quality and relevance.

Can you automate reports?

Yes. Data from compatible sources can be collected and transformed into recurring dashboards, summaries or performance reports.

How much does an AI automation cost?

The cost depends on the number of steps, connected platforms, complexity, security requirements and volume of data processed. A simple automation can be relatively affordable, while a complete custom system requires a detailed quotation.

Are there ongoing costs?

Possibly. Some tools charge monthly subscriptions or usage-based fees. These costs are explained before implementation.

Do you provide maintenance for automations?

Yes. Maintenance can include monitoring, updates, corrections and adjustments when connected platforms or business processes change.

How do we start an AI automation project?

We begin by reviewing your current process and identifying the specific task you want to improve. I then propose a workflow, the necessary tools, estimated costs and an implementation plan.
